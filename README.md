# My Little Morning

Student Name: Yaroslav Protsenko

## Project Overview

My Little Morning is a short interactive experience made in Godot. The project is about the beginning of my morning as a first-year game design student and the small routine that happens before going to university.

The game starts with the player waking up and questioning why they should even get out of bed and go to university when there are so many other things they could do instead. After this short dialogue, the player enters the kitchen and can explore the environment, interact with objects, listen to music, and complete a small tea-making mini-game.

The goal of the project was to create a personal and simple experience that feels close to my real life. I tried to show the small details of my morning, such as turning on music after waking up, looking at objects around the room, and slowly preparing myself to leave the house.

## Response to the Brief

The assignment was about creating an interactive experience based on a day in the life of a first-year game design student. I interpreted this idea through a small morning routine instead of showing a full day.

I wanted the project to feel personal, so most of the interactions are based on real habits and thoughts from my own life. For example, the music system is connected to the phone, and the player can open it like a small music app. This reflects how I often start my morning by listening to music.

The game also includes small object interactions around the kitchen. These are not just random comments, but reactions based on how I might actually feel in the morning. For example, even if I like a book, I may still react negatively to it early in the morning because not many people want to start their day by reading. The Steam Deck interaction has a similar idea: I may want to play something, but there is never enough time before university.

## Controls

- `WASD` - Move
- `Q` - Open the phone / music menu
- `Right Mouse Button` - Interact with objects while looking at them
- `Space` - Pour tea during the tea mini-game

The camera cannot be freely rotated with the mouse. Instead, the movement works more like an old-style crawler, where the player and camera direction change depending on the movement key that is pressed.

## Gameplay Structure

The project has a short but complete gameplay flow:

1. **Opening Dialogue / Menu**

   The game begins with a small dialogue system that also works as a creative main menu. Instead of a normal Play or Quit button, the player chooses whether they want to get out of bed or stay there.

2. **Kitchen Exploration**

   After choosing to start the day, the player enters the kitchen. The player can walk around, look at objects, and interact with them by hovering the mouse over them and pressing the right mouse button.

3. **Object Interactions**

   Different objects have short personal comments. These interactions help show the player's mood in the morning and make the space feel more connected to real life.

4. **Phone and Music System**

   The player can open the phone by pressing `Q`. The phone works as a small music interface. It shows the name of the current track and the author, which also works as a natural way to credit the music inside the game.

5. **Tea Mini-Game**

   The main gameplay moment is a tea-pouring mini-game. A kettle moves from left to right, and the player has to press `Space` when the kettle is above the cup. If the timing is correct, tea is poured into the cup.

6. **Mini-Game Result**

   The result depends on the player's accuracy. If the player gets less than 80%, the game gives a more negative comment about being careless or messy. If the player gets 80% or higher, the player successfully starts the morning in a better mood.

7. **Leaving the House**

   After completing the tea mini-game successfully, the player can interact with the door and leave the house to go to university.

## Visual and Aesthetic Direction

The project is built around a small personal morning space. I wanted the environment to feel simple, readable, and connected to an ordinary student routine.

The main focus was not to create a large level, but to make a small location feel meaningful through interaction. The kitchen works as the main space where the player can explore objects, listen to music, make tea, and slowly move from waking up to leaving the house.

The phone UI was also important because I wanted the music to feel like it exists naturally inside the world rather than just playing in the background.

## Development Reflection

This project helped me understand how small personal details can become interactive game mechanics. Instead of creating a large or complicated game, I focused on making a short experience that feels complete and connected to a specific mood.

The most important part of the project was the tea mini-game because it gave the player a clear gameplay task inside an otherwise simple morning routine. It also helped the project feel more like a playable experience instead of only a walking simulator.

I also enjoyed making the phone and music system because it allowed me to connect real-life habits with the game world. It made the project feel more personal and also gave me a clean way to show music credits without breaking immersion.

Overall, the project taught me more about building a complete small game in Godot, including interaction systems, UI, mini-games, player controls, and scene flow.
